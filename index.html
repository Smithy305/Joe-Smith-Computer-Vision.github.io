I am currently pursuing my PhD at Newcastle University, where I research Image Formation and Digitisation Bias in Fine-Grained Classification Tasks. My work is conducted in collaboration with Procter & Gamble, focusing on how image capture and processing methods impact the performance of machine learning models in recognising subtle differences between similar classes.

My research interests lie at the intersection of deep learning, computer vision, and the challenges posed by real-world data. I am passionate about exploring innovative solutions to improve the accuracy and robustness of fine-grained image classification systems.

Outside of my academic work, I enjoy watching and following basketball, with a particular interest in the analytics side of the sport. I also find relaxation in listening to music, which helps me stay creative and inspired.
